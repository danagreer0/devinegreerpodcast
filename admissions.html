College Admissions

Guidance for navigating the college search and application process.

1. Creating a Schools List

  • Consider location, distance from home, and climate.
  • Think about school size — small, medium, or large campuses.
  • Research academic programs and majors offered.
  • Look at campus culture, diversity, and student life.
  • Include a mix of reach, match, and safety schools.

2. Majors & Minors

  • Explore majors early — look at required courses and career paths.
  • Consider adding a minor to complement your interests.
  • Remember: many students change majors, and that’s normal.

3. Touring College Campuses

  • Visit during the school year to see real campus life.
  • Talk to current students about their experiences.
  • Explore dorms, dining halls, and academic buildings.
  • Take notes — campuses start to blend together.

4. Scholarships

  • Apply early — many scholarships have fall deadlines.
  • Look for local scholarships in your community.
  • Use scholarship databases and school financial aid pages.
  • Write strong personal statements that highlight your story.

5. Quarter vs Semester Systems

  • Quarter system: Faster pace, more classes per year.
  • Semester system: Longer terms, deeper focus on each class.
  • Consider which learning style fits you best.

6. On‑Campus Resources

  • Academic advising and tutoring centers.
  • Mental health and wellness services.
  • Career centers for internships and job prep.
  • Cultural centers and student support programs.

7. Black Student Clubs & Organizations

  • Black Student Union (BSU)
  • Cultural clubs and affinity groups
  • Academic and professional organizations
  • Community service and outreach groups

These spaces help students find community, support, and belonging.

8. Building Connections

  • Attend office hours and introduce yourself to professors.
  • Join clubs, teams, and campus organizations.
  • Connect with advisors and mentors early.
  • Build friendships with people who support your goals.
