College Athletics Recruitment Tips

Guidance for navigating the recruiting process as a student‑athlete.

1. Contacting College Coaches

  • Introduce yourself with your name, grad year, position, and GPA.
  • Attach highlight film or athletic resume.
  • Personalize each email — mention why you’re interested in their program.
  • Follow up every 1–2 weeks if you don’t hear back.
  • Be professional: use a simple email address and proper formatting.

2. NCAA Rules to Know

  • Understand contact periods, quiet periods, and dead periods.
  • Know when coaches are allowed to call, text, or email you.
  • Official vs. unofficial visits — what’s allowed and what’s not.
  • Academic eligibility requirements for each division.

3. Divisions & Types of Schools

  • Division I: Highest competition level, largest time commitment.
  • Division II: Balanced academics and athletics, partial scholarships.
  • Division III: No athletic scholarships, strong academic focus.
  • NAIA & JUCO: Great pathways for development and transfer opportunities.

4. NCAA Eligibility Center

  • Create an account early in high school.
  • Submit transcripts, test scores, and amateurism certification.
  • Track your core courses to stay eligible.

5. Training Tips

  • Build a consistent weekly training schedule.
  • Incorporate strength, conditioning, and recovery days.
  • Film your practices to track progress.
  • Work with coaches to identify weaknesses and goals.

6. Nutrition Tips

  • Fuel with balanced meals: protein, carbs, healthy fats.
  • Hydrate before, during, and after training.
  • Eat recovery meals within 30–60 minutes post‑workout.
  • Prioritize sleep — it’s part of your nutrition and recovery.
