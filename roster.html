Student Roster

Stories from Black women athletes at UCSB.

Amor Jones (she/her)

Major: Communications + Art History Minor

Year: Senior

Hometown: Los Angeles, CA

Favorite Artist: Tyler the Creator

Amor’s Story

I've been running track since I was 6 years old. I started off playing soccer, then my mother and coaches saw potential in track. I later became inspired by Flo Jo — her style, her confidence, and the way she owned the track.

I chose UCSB because of the beautiful location. My favorite part is the beach and the amazing people here. I'm part of the fashion club because I love anything fashion-related.

I want Black students at PWIs to know that it’s okay — my experience has been positive. Finding your people makes everything better.

Advice: Use the UCEN and Student Resource Building — there are so many resources students don’t take advantage of.

Favorite Courses: Greek Mythology and History of Photography.

Plans After Graduation: Stay in Santa Barbara, coach track, travel, and work.

Instagram: @amor_jones

Layo Ogunleye (she/her)

Major: Economics & Accounting + Communications

Year: Sophomore

Hometown: Fairfield, CA

Favorite Artists: PARTYNEXTDOOR, Frank Ocean, Ruger, SZA

Layo’s Story

I’ve always loved the arts — singing, dancing, flute, praise dance, choir. During quarantine, I learned to do my own hair and fell in love with protective styles.

I chose UCSB for a change of pace and the warm weather. I love the beach and the interconnected campus community.

I’m involved in the Nigerian Student Association, Black Student Union, and multiple internships focused on outreach and inclusivity. My identity as a Black student has shaped my leadership and purpose.

Advice

Research your major and be intentional with the spaces you enter. Know where to go for opportunities and put yourself out there.

Favorite Course: Philosophy 4 — Intro to Ethics.

Plans After Graduation: MBA, inclusive marketing, creative directing, and opening Black hair salons across the US.

Instagram: @_layyyooo · @hair.bylayo
